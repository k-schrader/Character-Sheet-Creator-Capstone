What is Dungeons and Dragons?

  • Dungeons and Dragons is a "table-top role-playing game". This means it is a game mostly of imagination!
  • Players create a fantasy character (maybe they can cast magic spells, maybe they can sword fight) and come together at the table.
  • One player -The Dungeon Master- creates the settings and events in their head and invites everyone else to interact with their story as their characters.
  • The players and DM roll dice and calculate scores to decide the outcome of their actions.
  • The game is as age appropriate as the players and Dungeon Master make it, since it is mostly imagined.
  • Dungeons and Dragons is a great exercise in creativity and group problem solving for your child!
  • The calculation of dice rolls is a fun and engaging math practice.
  • Though violence is not a necessary part of the game, mixing swords and monsters usually results in sword fighting. Parents should be aware of this and advise/monitor younger children as they see fit!
